Achievements

Champaran Satyagraha

This movement was against the indigo planters in Champaran, Bihar, who were exploiting farmers by forcing them to cultivate indigo and sell it at low prices.

Kheda Satyagraha

This movement was in support of farmers in Kheda, Gujarat, who were facing crop failures and high taxes.

Khilafat Movement

This movement was in solidarity with the Muslims of India who were protesting against the British government's decision to partition the Ottoman Empire.

Non-Cooperation Movement

This movement aimed at achieving Swaraj (self-rule) through non-cooperation with British institutions and laws. Gandhi called for boycotts of British goods, schools, and courts.

Movements

Train incident

While traveling from Durban to Pretoria, Gandhi was thrown off a train because he refused to give up his first-class seat, despite having a valid ticket. This humiliating experience ignited his anger and inspired him to begin his fight against racial discrimination and injustice in South Africa.

The salt March

One of Gandhi's most iconic acts of civil disobedience, the Salt March of 1930 was a 240-mile journey from Ahmedabad to the coastal town of Dandi.

The Quit India Movement

Launched in 1942, the Quit India Movement was a mass protest aimed at forcing the British to leave India. Gandhi called for "Do or Die," urging Indians to engage in nonviolent civil disobedience.
